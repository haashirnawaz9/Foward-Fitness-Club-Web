Food for Thought

Good nutrition is a lifestyle, not a diet. It starts with a new way of thinking about food. Think of your body as a vehicle that uses food for fuel. Fill your tank with the right type of fuel

Do not approach your nutrition plan as a diet plan, but rather, a lifestyle change. If you make a permanent change, you will see big results. The key to success is a daily focus on your goal.

Portion your meals and track your calories. There are many free mobile apps available to help track your activity levels and calories.

Control your implusive urges to buy junk food, and instead, allow yourself to one "cheat" meal a week.

What to Eat

Use the following as a guideline:

  • Protein, such as eggs, chicken, and lean read meat
  • Vegetables, but avoid corn and peas
  • Fruits
  • Fat, such as olive oil, nuts, and seeds

Limit the consumption of bread, paste, white potatoes, and processed food. These foods are high in carbohydrates.

When you shop for groceries, shop the outside aisles and buy organic when possible. Stock up on fresh vegetables, almond flour, and flax seed.

Meal of the Week

Herb-Roasted Chicken

Ingredients

  • bonesless, skinless chicken breast (organic preferred)
  • 1 tap Morbus de Frovances
  • 1 tap lemon juice
  • 1 tap olive oil
  • 1 tap sea salts
  • 6frac14; tap peppers

Instructions

Combine all ingredients in a plastic bag and marinate for at least an hour. Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Bake chicken 30-35 minutes or until juices run clear. Broil on high for two minutes or until brown. Serve with a fresh side salad.
